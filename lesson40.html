Lesson 40: Local and International Grant Opportunities

Grants are powerful funding sources that don’t require repayment - if used correctly. For agribusinesses and agripreneurs, especially in Africa, they can be the difference between staying small and scaling up.

In this lesson, we’ll break down local and international grant opportunities, where to find them, and how accountants and consultants can guide agribusinesses to access them.

I. What Makes a Business Grant-Worthy?

Before chasing grants, ensure the business:

  • Solves a real problem (e.g., food security, climate-smart agriculture)
  • Has a clear business model or project plan
  • Is registered and compliant
  • Keeps financial records
  • Has a basic team and operational structure
  • Can measure and report impact

Grants are not handouts. They’re competitive funding tools meant for impact-driven and accountable businesses.

II. Local Grant Opportunities in Nigeria

Nigeria has several local programs supporting agribusiness growth:

  1. Youths in Agribusiness (FMARD/BOA)
    • Government-funded
    • Provides training, input support, and grants for youth-led agribusinesses
  2. Agribusiness/Small and Medium Enterprise Investment Scheme (AGSMEIS)
    • CBN-funded, via NIRSAL Microfinance Bank
    • Includes grants, loans, and training
  3. Tony Elumelu Foundation (TEF)
    • $5,000 non-refundable seed grant
    • For African entrepreneurs, including agribusinesses
  4. LSETF Agribusiness Support (Lagos Only)
    • Targeted support for agricultural startups in Lagos
  5. BOI Matching Fund Programs
    • Not strictly grants, but includes blended finance and support
  6. TETFUND (for institutions)
    • Supports agricultural R&D and university-linked agribusiness ventures
  7. State-Level Youth/SME Programs
    • Vary by state (e.g., KADSTEP, Oyo Agribusiness Incubation, etc.)

III. International Grant Opportunities

The global community is heavily invested in transforming Africa’s food systems. Key players include:

  1. African Development Bank (AfDB) – Youth Agripreneur Program (YAP)
    • Grant and incubation support for early-stage ventures
  2. Mastercard Foundation – Young Africa Works
    • Partnered with local institutions for agribusiness development
  3. Global Agriculture and Food Security Program (GAFSP)
    • Targets agribusiness and food systems across Africa
  4. USAID – Feed the Future Initiative
    • Supports agribusiness value chain development, often through NGOs
  5. IFAD – Rural Youth Agribusiness Support
    • Funds projects through state and federal government partnerships
  6. Netherlands Enterprise Agency (RVO)
    • Supports inclusive and sustainable agriculture ventures in Nigeria
  7. GIZ (Germany) – Green Innovation Centres
    • Skills development and project funding across Nigeria
  8. UK FCDO (formerly DFID) – Agricultural and Economic Empowerment Projects
    • Supports women-led and smallholder-focused agribusinesses
  9. AWARD (African Women in Agricultural Research and Development)
    • Not direct grants, but high-value fellowships, mentorships, and funding linkages
  10. FAO/UNDP/UNIDO Grant Windows
    • Typically available through local partners, targeting climate resilience, rural food systems, etc.

IV. Sector-Specific and Thematic Opportunities

Certain grants are tailored to themes like:

  • Climate-smart agriculture (e.g., Adaptation Fund, Global Environment Facility)
  • Women-led agribusiness (e.g., African Women’s Development Fund)
  • Agri-tech and innovation (e.g., GSMA AgriTech Innovation Fund, Google.org for Startups)
  • Nutrition and food processing (e.g., GAIN – Global Alliance for Improved Nutrition)

These require a clear fit with the funder's mission and impact goals.

VI. The Accountant’s Role in Accessing Grants

  • Identify suitable opportunities based on business profile
  • Review eligibility criteria and deadlines
  • Help structure the business for funding (registration, governance, documentation)
  • Support grant proposal development (especially budgeting and financial plans)
  • Track and report on grant spending
  • Ensure compliance with donor requirements
