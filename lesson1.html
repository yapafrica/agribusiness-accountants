Lesson 1: Overview of Agribusiness in Nigeria

Agribusiness in Nigeria goes beyond farming. It is a complex and dynamic sector that spans production, processing, distribution, marketing, and export of agricultural goods. It’s one of the largest employers of labour in the country and a key driver of economic growth, food security, and rural development.

The Size and Scope of Agribusiness

Agriculture contributes approximately 25% of Nigeria’s GDP, making it the largest contributor outside the oil sector. According to the National Bureau of Statistics (NBS), over 70% of Nigerians are engaged in agriculture, either directly or indirectly. This includes crop farming, livestock, fisheries, agro-processing, and distribution.

Agribusiness also fuels other sectors - transportation, retail, exports, manufacturing, and even financial services—through value chain linkages. From a business perspective, it’s not just about food production; it’s about logistics, supply chains, risk management, and financing—all of which require financial expertise.

The Untapped Potential

Despite its scale, agribusiness in Nigeria remains largely informal, under-financed, and poorly documented. Many players operate without proper accounting records, financial planning, or access to institutional funding.

Where Accountants Fit In

This is where you come in. As an accountant, you have the tools to bridge this gap.

Agribusinesses need financial systems that track income, costs, assets, and liabilities. They need accurate reporting to access funding. They need advisory services to become sustainable and scale-ready. Your role is not just bookkeeping - it’s strategic. You can help them become grant-ready, investor-ready, and compliant with tax and regulatory requirements.

  • Over 80% of agribusinesses are micro or small enterprises.
  • Less than 5% of them can produce audited financial statements.
  • Many miss out on grants, loans, and investment opportunities simply because they lack proper financial documentation.

Current Trends Shaping Agribusiness

  • Digital Agriculture: Tech startups are entering the sector, digitizing everything from farm records to supply chains.
  • Youth Involvement: A growing number of young entrepreneurs are entering agribusiness with new models and digital tools.
  • Funding Access: Government and development partners are increasing funding pools for agribusinesses - especially women-led and green ventures.
  • Global Trade: There’s increased demand for Nigerian agro-products like sesame seeds, cocoa, and ginger in international markets.
