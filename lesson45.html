Lesson 45: Tools and Templates for Agribusiness Consulting

When working with agribusiness clients - whether smallholder farmers or processing startups - having the right tools and templates can help you provide clarity, structure, and value from day one. This lesson will walk you through essential resources you need in your consulting toolkit.

You don’t need to be complex - what you need is to be practical and efficient.

I. Basic Tools Every Agribusiness Consultant Should Have

1. Financial Record Templates

Many agribusiness owners don’t keep structured records. Start by introducing simple tools:

  • Daily Expense Tracker (paper or Excel)
  • Sales Register (with date, item sold, quantity, unit price, total)
  • Petty Cash Book
  • Inventory Sheet (for inputs like feed, seeds, fertilizer)

These help clients gradually develop a culture of documentation.

2. Budgeting Template

  • Monthly and seasonal budget templates
  • Line items for inputs, labor, transportation, packaging, and marketing
  • Comparison columns: Planned vs. Actual

Use this to help clients forecast costs and control spending.

3. Cash Flow Forecast Tool

  • Inflows (sales, loans, grants)
  • Outflows (input purchases, salaries, transport)
  • Monthly cash balance tracker

This helps farmers and agribusinesses plan ahead and avoid cash shortages during low seasons.

II. Analytical Templates

1. Cost-Benefit Analysis Sheet

  • For comparing new investments (e.g., should I start fish farming or expand poultry?)
  • Captures expected income, total cost, ROI
  • Helps your client avoid emotional decisions and invest wisely

2. Break-Even Analysis Tool

  • Know the minimum sales needed to cover costs
  • Useful for crop production, processing, or livestock
  • Reveals when and how a business starts to make profit

3. Farm Performance Dashboard (Basic Excel)

  • Monthly income/expense summary
  • Top-selling products
  • Profitability per product or unit
  • Inventory snapshot

Even a simple dashboard like this can help clients “see their business” at a glance.

III. Tax and Compliance Templates

1. Tax Computation Worksheet

  • Annual turnover
  • Allowable deductions
  • Tax rates based on business type
  • Helps in PAYE, VAT, and CIT estimations

2. Compliance Checklist

  • NAFDAC registration (if producing food)
  • SON standards (if packaging)
  • Business registration
  • Regulatory deadlines

Use this to advise clients on what licenses they need, and when.

IV. Grant and Investment Tools

1. Grant Proposal Outline

  • Executive summary
  • Problem statement
  • Solution and business model
  • Budget
  • Impact and scalability

You can adapt this structure to help clients apply for grants or pitch to NGOs.

2. Investor Pitch Deck Template

  • Introduction
  • Market opportunity
  • Business model
  • Financial projections
  • Ask (funding request)

Ideal for agribusinesses ready to grow or seeking angel investors.

V. Reporting Templates

1. Monthly Financial Report

  • Income statement format
  • Cash movement summary
  • Commentary (what went well, what needs improvement)

Simple reporting builds discipline - and prepares clients for future funding opportunities.

2. Annual Performance Review

  • Profit/loss summary
  • ROI by product
  • Recommendations for the next cycle
  • Can be used as a consultant’s final deliverable

VI. Tools You Can Use to Build or Customize Templates

  • Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets – For editable templates
  • Google Forms – To collect daily records from clients using smartphones
  • Canva or PowerPoint – For pitch decks or strategy presentations
  • PDF Editors – To package reports and templates professionally

You don’t need to code. Just know how to adapt tools to each client’s level.
