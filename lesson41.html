Lesson 41: Building an Agribusiness Consulting Career

The future of agriculture in Africa is business-oriented, data-driven, and consultant-dependent. Agribusinesses increasingly need experts who understand both their operations and how to structure and scale them - and that’s where accountants come in.

If you’re a young accounting student or professional, the agribusiness sector offers a rich, untapped field where your skills can drive real impact.

I. Why Consider a Career in Agribusiness Consulting?

  • Large market size: Nigeria has over 70 million hectares of arable land. Agriculture contributes over 25% of GDP.
  • Under-served businesses: Many agribusinesses operate informally, lacking sound financial systems or advisory support.
  • Rising donor interest: Billions of dollars flow into agricultural development and food systems yearly.
  • Versatility: The sector spans crop production, livestock, aquaculture, processing, logistics, exports, and more.

As a consultant, you can work with:

  • Individual farmers
  • Cooperatives and aggregators
  • Processors and manufacturers
  • NGOs and grant-funded projects
  • Government programs and policy think tanks

II. Key Skills You Need to Build

  1. Accounting and Financial Management
    • Strong grasp of bookkeeping, budgeting, and reporting
    • Familiarity with agri-specific statements and costing
  2. Understanding of Agricultural Systems
    • Production cycles, seasonality, input-output relationships
    • Knowledge of agribusiness models and value chains
  3. Regulations and Compliance
    • Tax rules, licensing, food safety standards, and IFRS for agriculture
  4. Business Advisory and Strategy
    • Investment readiness, pricing, feasibility studies, grant proposal budgeting
  5. Communication and Training
    • Ability to simplify complex ideas for farmers and entrepreneurs
    • Designing training materials and leading workshops
  6. Digital Tools
    • Excel, Power BI, QuickBooks, mobile record-keeping apps
    • Exposure to AgTech and data systems (e.g., remote sensing, mobile data capture)

III. Steps to Launch Your Career

Step 1: Learn the Fundamentals

  • Complete this course
  • Explore free resources from FMARD, IFAD, AGRA, and Agribusiness TV
  • Study real-life cases and financial reports of agribusinesses

Step 2: Gain Practical Exposure

  • Volunteer with agricultural cooperatives or agri-focused NGOs
  • Intern with farms or processors
  • Offer pro bono advisory services to small farmers to build experience

Step 3: Build a Portfolio

  • Document every project, training, or advisory session you do
  • Track impact - how did your advice improve the business?

Step 4: Create a Brand

  • Set up a LinkedIn profile that highlights your agribusiness consulting focus
  • Write articles, speak at student forums, host webinars, share insights

Step 5: Keep Learning and Growing

  • Join communities like YAP Innovation Lab, Her MSMEGrow Lab, and local agric hubs
  • Attend agritech fairs, pitch events, and development sector meetups
  • Subscribe to newsletters from IFPRI, CTA, Agrilinks

IV. Opportunities to Monetize Your Expertise

  • Retainers with cooperatives or processors to handle accounting and compliance
  • Training programs for farmer groups or NGOs
  • Grant consulting and budget preparation services
  • Business setup and formalization for informal agri-ventures
  • Feasibility studies and investment proposals
  • Digital tool implementation (setting up bookkeeping or inventory systems)

You don’t have to wait to be hired - create your own practice, even as a student. Start small, build credibility, and grow.
