Lesson 2: The Role of Accountants in Agribusiness

Many agribusinesses in Nigeria are struggling, not because they lack land or crops, but because they lack structure, strategy, and financial clarity. This is exactly where the accountant steps in - not as just as some accountant, but as a trusted business advisor.

1. Financial Record-Keeping and Reporting

Agribusinesses, especially smallholder farms and agro-processors, often operate informally. They rarely keep proper records of income, expenses, inventories, or capital. Without these, it's hard to track performance or attract funding.

As an accountant, your first role is to set up simple, reliable financial systems:

  • Income and expense tracking
  • Inventory and asset management
  • Periodic profit/loss analysis
  • Basic financial statements (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow)

These records form the foundation for business decision-making.

2. Funding and Investment Readiness

Agribusinesses need funding to scale, and funders-banks, investors, and grant bodies-require clean, verifiable financial information.

Your role includes:

  • Preparing financial projections
  • Developing investment pitches with financial backing
  • Ensuring due diligence readiness (bank statements, statements of account, tax filings, etc.)
  • Creating grant-compliant budgets and reports

In short, you make the business “investment-ready.”

3. Costing and Pricing Strategies

Many farmers and agro-entrepreneurs don’t know the true cost of their operations. That leads to poor pricing, thin margins, or losses.

You can help them:

  • Calculate unit costs (per kg, per bag, per crate)
  • Identify cost leakages
  • Set realistic, profitable prices
  • Analyze seasonal profitability and guide procurement decisions

This is critical for long-term sustainability.

4. Compliance with Tax and Regulatory Bodies

Even small agribusinesses are subject to tax laws, licensing requirements, and industry regulations.

You help them stay compliant with:

  • Company Income Tax (CIT) or Personal Income Tax (PIT)
  • VAT, Withholding Tax, and other statutory deductions
  • Regulatory filings with NAFDAC, SON, FMARD, and others
  • Annual returns and audit preparation

Non-compliance leads to fines and lost business opportunities - your work prevents this.

5. Strategic Advisory

The best accountants don’t just record, they advise.

You bring insight into:

  • Cash flow planning for seasonal operations
  • Loan management and repayment strategies
  • Expansion planning (e.g. when to buy new equipment)
  • Partnerships, cooperatives, and mergers

As the business grows, you become a key part of its leadership.
