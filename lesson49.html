Lesson 49: Case Study – Grant Compliance in Rural Farming Project

Grants are powerful tools for driving agricultural transformation, especially in rural communities. But without proper oversight and financial discipline, many promising projects collapse - either due to mismanagement or failure to comply with donor guidelines.

This lesson presents a real-world inspired case study where an accountant ensured full compliance, accountability, and sustainability in a rural farming project funded by an international donor.

Project Background

  • Project Title: Community Maize Empowerment Project (CMEP)
  • Location: Gboko LGA, Benue State
  • Donor: African Agricultural Transformation Fund (AATF)
  • Grant Size: $65,000 (approx. ₦50 million)
  • Beneficiaries: 60 smallholder maize farmers (40 women, 20 men)
  • Duration: 12 months
  • Objective: Provide inputs, extension services, and market access to increase maize yield and income by 200%

Initial Challenges

Within the first three months, the project encountered major risks:

  • No dedicated accountant; the project lead was handling finances informally
  • Poor documentation of expenses and no clear chart of accounts
  • Lack of internal control systems - receipts missing, cash withdrawals without justification
  • Growing concerns from the donor about transparency and proper use of funds

Donor support was at risk of suspension.

The Intervention: Deploying a Grant Compliance Accountant

A young accountant, trained in nonprofit financial management, was brought in as a project compliance officer. Their first step was to assess and implement key accountability measures.

Step 1: Structuring the Financial System

  • Created a chart of accounts aligned with the donor’s reporting format
  • Set up a basic accounting system using Excel and later transitioned to QuickBooks
  • Opened a dedicated project account to separate grant funds from personal or other business expenses
  • Introduced a payment request form and petty cash voucher system

Step 2: Financial Controls & Documentation

  • Required all expenses above ₦50,000 to be pre-approved and backed with receipts
  • Trained the field coordinator and procurement officer on basic bookkeeping and expense documentation
  • Established weekly cash reconciliation and monthly financial reporting

Step 3: Donor Compliance & Reporting

  • Carefully reviewed the grant agreement, mapping out compliance requirements including spending caps, procurement rules, and reporting timelines
  • Generated quarterly donor reports (narrative and financial), including variance analysis
  • Introduced activity-based budgeting, linking each expense directly to project milestones

Step 4: Outcome Measurement & Audit Readiness

  • Worked with the monitoring and evaluation (M&E) team to align financial data with impact data (e.g., cost per farmer trained, input cost per hectare)
  • Prepared the team for an external audit by compiling a donor-ready documentation pack: vouchers, receipts, reports, bank statements

Impact After 12 Months

  • Project delivered on all KPIs - farmers reported an average yield increase of 230%, and 75% increased income by at least 2x
  • The donor praised the transparent financial management, releasing an additional $25,000 extension grant
  • The project’s financial reports were used as a model for other grantees in the donor’s network
  • The accountant was hired full-time as a Finance and Compliance Lead for future AATF projects

Lessons for Young Accounting Professionals

  • Grants are not free money. They come with strict rules, and it’s the accountant’s job to uphold them.
  • Financial discipline builds trust. The project almost lost funding due to weak systems - not bad intentions.
  • You don’t need fancy tools to start. Excel and strong internal controls can take you a long way when managed professionally.
  • Your role is beyond bookkeeping. You are part of the project’s success, helping it deliver impact and secure future funding.
