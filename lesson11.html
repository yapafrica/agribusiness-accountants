Lesson 11: Crop Production and Processing

Crops are the heartbeat of agriculture in Nigeria — from staple foods like maize and rice to high-value exports like cocoa, cashew, and sesame. But growing crops is only half the story. Processing them adds value, creates jobs, and opens up more opportunities for income and investment. As an accountant in agribusiness, understanding both production and processing is essential to support smarter decision-making and profitability.

What is Crop Production?

Crop production involves all the activities from land preparation to harvest. It includes:

  1. Land Preparation – Clearing, ploughing, ridging
  2. Seed Selection and Planting – Choosing the right varieties for yield and climate
  3. Crop Management – Fertilizing, weeding, irrigation, and pest control
  4. Harvesting – Picking crops at the right time to preserve quality

Nigeria’s most cultivated crops include:

  • Cereals – Maize, rice, sorghum
  • Tubers – Cassava, yam, potatoes
  • Legumes – Soybeans, groundnuts
  • Cash Crops – Cocoa, oil palm, sesame, cashew
  • Vegetables and Fruits – Tomatoes, onions, mangoes, bananas

Each crop type has different cycles, costs, and storage needs. As an accountant, your job is to understand these differences when costing, projecting revenue, or advising on investments.

Understanding Crop Processing

Processing is the transformation of raw crops into semi-finished or finished products. This could be as simple as drying and packaging or as complex as turning cassava into starch or cocoa into chocolate.

Examples:

  • Cassava → Garri, Fufu, Starch, Ethanol
  • Maize → Flour, Animal Feed, Corn Oil
  • Cocoa → Cocoa Butter, Powder, Chocolate
  • Tomatoes → Paste, Sauce

Processing adds value, extends shelf life, and opens up export opportunities. It’s where agribusinesses can move from small profits to scalable ventures.

Why Accountants Should Care

Crop production and processing involve different types of costs and revenues. Here’s where you come in:

  • Budgeting – Plan for input costs, labor, and processing machinery
  • Cost Analysis – Break down cost per hectare or per tonne processed
  • Cash Flow Planning – Production may have long cycles; processing adds more working capital needs
  • Break-even Analysis – Know how much volume is needed to recover processing investments
  • Profit Margin Review – Processing often improves margins — but only if efficiently done
  • Inventory Tracking – Monitor produce at every stage to reduce losses and improve traceability

Real-World Example

A smallholder farmer produces 10 tonnes of cassava at N40,000 per tonne (N400,000 total revenue). But if she processes it into garri and sells at N120 per kg (10 tonnes = approx. 2,500 kg of garri), she could earn N300,000+ more — if managed well.

This is the kind of value addition that makes agribusiness more bankable and sustainable.

Emerging Opportunities in Crop Processing

  • Mobile processing units for remote farmers
  • Solar dryers for tomatoes, pepper, and fruits
  • Mini rice mills in rural areas
  • Packaging innovations that meet export standards

Accountants can help businesses assess the feasibility, profitability, and risks of such investments.
