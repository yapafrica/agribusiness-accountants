Lesson 42: Designing Services for Agribusiness Clients

One of the most important things every accountant venturing into agribusiness consulting must understand is this: your clients don’t just need accounting - they need solutions.

Agribusinesses come in all shapes and sizes. From a smallholder poultry farmer to a large-scale tomato processor, their financial literacy, pain points, and growth stage vary. So, how do you design services they’ll actually pay for?

Let’s break it down.

I. Understand the Client Before Designing Services

Start by assessing:

  • Business size and structure (individual, cooperative, registered company, etc.)
  • Level of formality (informal, semi-formal, or fully structured)
  • Pain points (e.g., no records, irregular cash flow, poor pricing, tax pressure)
  • Growth goals (accessing grants, bank loans, investor readiness, export)

Your goal is to fit the service to the client - not the other way around.

II. Core Service Areas for Agribusiness Clients

Here are practical services you can offer, grouped by needs:

  1. Record-Keeping and Bookkeeping
    • Set up basic accounting systems (manual or digital)
    • Train staff or owners on daily record-keeping
    • Monthly transaction reconciliation and cash flow monitoring
  2. Financial Reporting and Compliance
    • Prepare regular income statements and balance sheets
    • Compute and file taxes (PAYE, WHT, VAT, CIT)
    • Generate audit-ready reports for banks, investors, or regulators
  3. Costing and Pricing Advisory
    • Help determine cost per unit (e.g., per kg of poultry feed or crate of tomatoes)
    • Identify pricing leaks and untracked costs
    • Set sustainable pricing strategies based on cost structure
  4. Budgeting and Forecasting
    • Develop seasonal or annual budgets
    • Set financial targets based on production cycles
    • Scenario planning for climate, price shocks, or input changes
  5. Grant and Funding Support
    • Develop grant-ready financial reports
    • Draft financial sections of proposals
    • Support with financial reporting for donor compliance
  6. Investment Readiness and Business Plans
    • Create compelling financial projections
    • Structure the business legally and financially
    • Support due diligence and data room preparation
  7. Training and Capacity Building
    • Train farmers or staff on basic finance
    • Organize webinars or in-person workshops
    • Provide guides/checklists tailored to agri-operations
  8. Technology Onboarding
    • Introduce digital tools (mobile apps, POS, inventory systems)
    • Help set up simple Excel dashboards
    • Teach data entry and report generation

III. Sample Service Packages

It helps to bundle your services for clarity and pricing. Here are a few examples:

  • Starter Package (For Smallholder Farmers)
    • Manual record book setup
    • Monthly check-in and cash flow review
    • Basic tax advice
  • Growth Package (For SMEs & Processors)
    • Full bookkeeping setup (Excel or app-based)
    • Quarterly financial statements
    • Costing & pricing analysis
    • Compliance checklist
  • Scale Package (For Investor-Ready Ventures)
    • Complete business and financial plan
    • IFRS-compliant statements
    • Tax compliance and optimization
    • Grant proposal financial support

IV. Pricing Your Services

Be mindful of what your target segment can afford. You can:

  • Offer tiered pricing (monthly/quarterly)
  • Use retainers for ongoing clients
  • Provide one-off services like proposal budgets or tax computation
  • Offer discounted student-led or intern support models to pilot your services while building a team

Remember, clarity is key - use checklists, templates, and visuals when pitching your services.

V. Positioning Yourself as a Solution Provider

  • Start with education: Most agribusinesses don’t know what they don’t know. Break things down in simple terms.
  • Use examples: “With proper cost tracking, your profit per crate increased by 20%.”
  • Be outcome-driven: Show how your service helps them earn more, save more, or stay compliant.
