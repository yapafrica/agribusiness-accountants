Lesson 14: Agro-processing and Food Manufacturing

Agriculture doesn’t end at the farm gate. The real value – financially and nutritionally – often comes after harvest, through processing and manufacturing. That’s where agro-processing steps in, transforming raw agricultural produce into semi-finished or finished goods like flour, juice, oil, spices, dairy, and packaged snacks.

This sector plays a major role in reducing post-harvest losses, increasing farmer incomes, and creating jobs – from factory workers to logistics and marketing professionals. For accountants, agro-processing businesses need sound cost tracking, inventory controls, and compliance with regulations.

What is Agro-processing?

Agro-processing involves all operations that convert raw farm products (grains, fruits, vegetables, meat, dairy, etc.) into usable, storable, or marketable products. It can be as simple as drying pepper or as complex as producing bottled tomato paste or cereal.

Why is Agro-processing Important in Nigeria?

  • Post-harvest losses in Nigeria exceed 40% in some commodities due to poor storage and lack of processing
  • It enables value addition, which multiplies the price of farm products (e.g., cassava into garri, maize into cereal)
  • Drives industrialization and import substitution
  • Boosts exports of processed products, generating foreign exchange
  • Encourages rural economic development and job creation

Food Manufacturing: Scaling from Kitchen to Factory

Food manufacturing goes beyond small-scale processing. It includes packaging, preservation, branding, quality control, and regulatory compliance. It’s where entrepreneurs build brands and scale.

Examples:

  • Packaged plantain chips with NAFDAC numbers
  • Bottled juices or drinks made from hibiscus (zobo), ginger, tiger nuts
  • Locally made noodles, flour blends, seasoning cubes

Accounting Considerations in Agro-processing

  1. Costing Systems
    • Track raw material costs, direct labor, utilities, packaging, and overhead
    • Use job costing or process costing depending on production flow
  2. Inventory Management
    • Monitor raw materials, work-in-progress (WIP), and finished goods
    • Maintain batch records for traceability and compliance
  3. Pricing and Profit Margins
    • Help determine break-even points
    • Factor in distribution and retail margins
  4. Regulatory Compliance
    • Ensure accurate record-keeping for NAFDAC, SON, tax authorities
    • Support with product registration documentation and tax filings
  5. Quality and Waste Accounting
    • Track production losses and inefficiencies
    • Allocate costs of rejects, packaging waste, or spoilage
  6. Tax Implications
    • Many agro-processors qualify for tax holidays or incentives under pioneer status or BOI/NEPC programs

Opportunities for Accountants

Agro-processors need accounting professionals to:

  • Set up robust bookkeeping systems
  • Cost their products correctly
  • Budget for equipment purchases or expansion
  • Secure financing or grants with solid financial statements
  • Navigate regulatory landscapes with clear documentation

Whether it's a garri factory in Ogun or a yogurt brand in Lagos, there's room to add value through accurate numbers and strategic financial advice.
