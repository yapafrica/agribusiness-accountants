Lesson 10: Use of Technology in Agribusiness (AgTech)

Agribusiness is no longer about hoes, cutlasses, and guesswork. Across Nigeria and the rest of Africa, technology is transforming how food is grown, processed, distributed, and accounted for. This revolution is known as AgTech — agricultural technology.

As an accountant in this space, you don't need to be a tech expert, but you must understand how AgTech tools work, how they affect costs and revenues, and how to guide agribusiness owners on adopting them smartly.

What is AgTech?

AgTech refers to the use of modern technologies — software, hardware, and digital tools — to improve agricultural practices and agribusiness operations.

This includes:

  • Mobile apps
  • Drones
  • Sensors and IoT (Internet of Things)
  • Artificial intelligence (AI)
  • Blockchain
  • Digital platforms for finance, markets, and records

AgTech is helping farmers and agribusinesses become more productive, efficient, and profitable.

Examples of AgTech Solutions in Nigeria

  1. Hello Tractor – A shared-economy platform that allows farmers to rent tractors and monitors their usage remotely.
  2. ThriveAgric – Connects smallholder farmers to finance, inputs, and off-takers through a digital platform.
  3. Crop2Cash – Enables traceability and digital payments along the agricultural value chain.
  4. Releaf – Uses hardware and software to improve oil palm processing for food factories.
  5. FarmCrowdy – A platform that links investors with vetted farming projects and provides digital farm monitoring.

These innovations solve practical problems like access to machinery, capital, markets, and transparency.

Benefits of AgTech for Agribusinesses

  • Better decision-making through data and forecasting
  • Improved productivity from automation and smart inputs
  • Reduced losses through weather alerts, pest detection, and smart storage
  • Efficient records for audits, grants, and financial planning
  • Market access through e-commerce and logistics platforms
  • Financial inclusion by enabling digital wallets and loan applications

How Accountants Fit Into the AgTech Ecosystem

Accountants play a strategic role in helping agribusiness owners adopt the right tech by:

  • Evaluating costs vs. ROI for any tech solution
  • Budgeting for tech investment (e.g., sensors, farm software, logistics tracking tools)
  • Advising on digital record-keeping systems
  • Ensuring compatibility between tech tools and financial reporting
  • Tracking tech-driven improvements in yield, efficiency, and profitability
  • Preparing grant or investor documentation with solid data from AgTech systems

AgTech and Financial Reporting

With digital tools now generating farm data, accountants must align financial reporting with:

  • Real-time inventory updates
  • Automated yield data
  • Digitized payment trails (e.g., mobile money, USSD)
  • Cost allocations based on GPS-monitored farm activities

This data-driven approach strengthens transparency and accountability — two things that attract funders and investors.

Barriers to AgTech Adoption

  • Cost of devices or subscriptions
  • Limited internet access in rural areas
  • Low digital literacy among farmers
  • Resistance to change

As a professional advisor, you must consider these realities and help clients find practical, scalable solutions — sometimes starting with WhatsApp-based systems or Excel before jumping to more advanced tools.
