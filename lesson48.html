Lesson 48: Case Study – Export-Focused Agro-Processing

One of Nigeria’s greatest untapped assets is its agro-processing sector, especially for exports. While many agribusinesses struggle locally, those that position themselves for export earn more, access foreign exchange, and build more sustainable operations.

In this lesson, we examine how a cassava-processing business repositioned itself for export - and how an accountant played a pivotal role in making that happen.

Business Background

  • Business Name: AgroVista Processing Ltd.
  • Location: Aba, Abia State
  • Product: High-quality Cassava Flour (HQCF) and Garri
  • Current Capacity: 3 tonnes/day
  • Goal: Expand to 10 tonnes/day and secure international buyers
  • Challenge: Not export-compliant, lacking certifications, weak financial documentation

The Problem

Despite high demand for HQCF in Europe and the diaspora market, AgroVista couldn’t break into export. The issues included:

  • No NAVDAC or SON certification
  • No financial statements to back grant or investor applications
  • Manual production processes lacking consistency
  • No understanding of export documentation and compliance

The owner had received inquiries from three UK-based buyers through trade fairs but couldn’t move forward due to lack of structure.

The Consultant’s Role

A young accountant, recently certified and exploring agro-consulting, took up the challenge. Here’s how they helped reposition AgroVista:

Step 1: Financial Housekeeping

  • Reconstructed the past 24 months’ financials - revenues, cost of sales, wages, utilities, maintenance
  • Developed projected financials for the next 2 years
  • Prepared a formal financial statement pack: Income Statement, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow

This helped put the business in a position to attract financing.

Step 2: Compliance & Certification Support

  • Registered for NAVDAC certification and liaised with SON for product standard review
  • Advised on HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points) requirements
  • Documented Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for production to ensure consistency

The accountant worked with a food safety consultant to structure these processes clearly.

Step 3: Export Readiness & Strategy

  • Registered AgroVista on the NEPC Exporter Portal
  • Helped secure a Product Export Certificate
  • Assisted in opening a domiciliary account to receive foreign payments
  • Collaborated with a clearing agent to educate the client on freight, tariffs, and shipping timelines

Step 4: Investor & Grant Application Support

  • Prepared an investment pitch deck using their structured financials
  • Helped apply for the AFEX Export Expansion Facility Grant
  • Connected with diaspora networks looking to invest in agro-processing

The accountant emphasized ROI, profit margins, and scalability backed by data.

Results (After 9 Months)

  • Certified by NAVDAC and SON
  • First export batch of cassava flour (2 tonnes) shipped to the UK
  • Signed a 12-month supply agreement with a UK-based ethnic food distributor
  • Received ₦7.5 million investment from a diaspora angel investor
  • Increased processing capacity to 6 tonnes/day
  • Implemented monthly management reports to track performance

Lessons for Accounting Professionals

  • Export-readiness is 70% documentation. Without the right reports and certifications, great products stay stuck in local markets.
  • You are the structure they need. Agribusiness owners are often product-focused. You help them become process- and profit-focused.
  • Compliance attracts currency. Once AgroVista got certified and structured, both buyers and investors paid attention.
