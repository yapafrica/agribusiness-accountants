Lesson 46: Case Study – Poultry Business Turnaround

Poultry businesses are popular among small-scale agripreneurs in Nigeria, but many of them fail within the first year - not because the birds didn’t grow, but because the business wasn’t properly managed.

Let’s look at how one young accounting graduate helped a local poultry venture go from losses to profitability in just one production cycle.

Business Background

  • Business name: HopeFeathers Poultry
  • Location: Akure, Ondo State
  • Model: Broiler production – 500 birds per cycle
  • Owner: Ms. Joy, a civil servant running the farm part-time
  • Challenge: Continuous financial losses despite regular sales

The Problems Identified

When our consultant (a fresh accounting graduate volunteering in an NGO program) visited the farm, here’s what was discovered:

  • No Production Planning
    • Birds were purchased without a clear feeding or sales plan.
    • Ms. Joy couldn’t tell how many bags of feed were needed per cycle.
  • Poor Expense Tracking
    • All expenses were done by cash and not recorded.
    • Feed wastage was common, and the farmhand often made unapproved purchases.
  • Pricing Errors
    • Chickens were sold at ₦3,500 each - below break-even price.
    • Sales were made without weighing birds or factoring costs.
  • High Mortality
    • Over 12% mortality per cycle (60 birds lost on average).
    • No vaccination schedule or veterinary input.

Consulting Intervention

The consultant rolled up their sleeves and got practical:

Step 1: Introduced a Production & Feed Plan

  • Developed a broiler feed schedule (starter, grower, finisher) with quantities and expected weights.
  • Matched this against a 6-week budget per cycle.

Step 2: Setup of Simple Expense Tracker

  • Created a paper-based ledger with clear columns: date, item, quantity, amount, approved by.
  • Trained Ms. Joy and the farmhand to record everything, daily.

Step 3: Break-Even Analysis and Pricing Adjustment

  • Calculated the total cost per bird: ₦3,850 (including feed, vaccines, labor, mortality loss)
  • Recommended selling at ₦4,500 minimum to cover costs and earn a margin.

Step 4: Improved Bird Management

  • Connected Ms. Joy with a local vet.
  • Introduced vaccination tracking, proper brooding practices, and feed efficiency monitoring.

Step 5: Monthly Review Reports

  • Created monthly summary reports (sales, expenses, profits) using Google Sheets.
  • Sent digital copies to Ms. Joy via email - this helped her monitor progress remotely.

Results After One Cycle (7 Weeks)

  • Mortality dropped from 12% to 3%
  • Profit per bird increased to ₦500
  • Cycle profit: Over ₦200,000
  • Feed wastage dropped significantly
  • Sales shifted from panic to planned bulk sales

Key Lessons for Consultants

  • Know your numbers, but keep it simple. You’re working with busy entrepreneurs. Don’t overwhelm them - educate them.
  • Structure beats struggle. Just having a plan and basic reporting can turn a chaotic farm into a real business.
  • The best consultants are hands-on. The impact came because the accountant got involved, not just advised.
