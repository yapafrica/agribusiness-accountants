Lesson 15: Export-Oriented Agribusiness Ventures

Agriculture isn’t just local - it’s global. From hibiscus flowers sent to Mexico, to cashew nuts shipped to Vietnam, Nigerian agribusinesses are increasingly tapping into export markets. This space is loaded with opportunities but also comes with strict standards, documentation, and financial planning – areas where accountants can shine.

Why Export from Nigeria?

  • High global demand for agro commodities like sesame seeds, cocoa, ginger, shea butter, cashew, and hibiscus
  • Favorable climate for year-round production of tropical crops
  • Foreign exchange earnings help hedge against naira volatility
  • Government incentives (NEPC grants, export expansion grants, tax holidays)

In 2023, non-oil export earnings in Nigeria hit over $4.5 billion, with agro-commodities accounting for a significant portion. But the gap between potential and actual performance is still huge.

Export Models and Channels

  1. Direct Exporting – The agribusiness handles everything: sourcing, packaging, documentation, shipping
  2. Export via Aggregators – Sell to middlemen/export agents who consolidate and export
  3. Outgrower Schemes – Multiple farmers are coordinated to supply a central processing/exporting firm
  4. Joint Ventures – Collaboration with foreign partners or processors

Key Requirements for Export

  1. Product Quality
    • Must meet international phytosanitary and food safety standards
    • Grading, sorting, drying, and packaging must be done professionally
  2. Certifications & Compliance
    • NAQS (Nigeria Agricultural Quarantine Service) inspection
    • NXP (Nigeria Export Proceeds Form) from your bank
    • SONCAP, PHYTO Certificate, Certificate of Origin, etc.
    • For processed products: NAFDAC registration and barcode
  3. Logistics & Documentation
    • Accurate invoice, packing list, Bill of Lading
    • Export license and exporter registration with NEPC (Nigerian Export Promotion Council)

The Accountant’s Role in Export Ventures

  1. Costing & Pricing for International Markets
    • Factor in shipping, customs clearance, packaging, forex, and logistics
    • Calculate margins based on incoterms (e.g., FOB, CIF)
  2. Foreign Exchange Management
    • Track FX rates, manage domiciliary accounts
    • Ensure proper inflow reporting via NXP forms
  3. Financial Reporting
    • Generate export-friendly financials for grant applications or investor pitches
    • Monitor cash flow cycles (exports can be cash-heavy and delayed)
  4. Regulatory Compliance
    • Ensure all export documentation is in place
    • Advise on tax incentives and duty exemptions
  5. Access to Finance
    • Prepare required documentation for NEXIM Bank loans, pre-export financing, and NEPC support schemes

Opportunities in Export for Accountants

  • Support export cooperatives or agro-clusters with training and record-keeping systems
  • Help SMEs build export-grade bookkeeping practices
  • Build financial models for expansion into export markets
  • Offer compliance advisory for clients navigating new markets

Challenges to Watch Out For

  • Quality inconsistency due to poor post-harvest handling
  • Documentation errors that delay shipments
  • Unstable policies and FX restrictions
  • Logistics bottlenecks at Nigerian ports

But these are also problem areas that accountants and consultants can help solve – with the right knowledge and systems.
