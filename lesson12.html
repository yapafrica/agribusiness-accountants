Lesson 12: Livestock and Poultry in Agribusiness

While crops get a lot of attention in agriculture, livestock and poultry are just as important — especially for food security, nutrition, job creation, and export potential. In fact, livestock contributes over 30% of agricultural GDP in Nigeria, with poultry alone being one of the fastest-growing segments.

As an accountant in agribusiness, you need to understand how these enterprises operate, earn, spend, and scale. The business dynamics, risks, and revenue models differ significantly from crops.

What Falls Under Livestock Agribusiness?

Livestock includes the breeding, rearing, and processing of animals for food, labor, or other products. Key sectors in Nigeria:

  • Poultry: Chicken (broilers, layers), turkey, quail
  • Ruminants: Cattle (meat and dairy), goats, sheep
  • Piggery: Pork production
  • Fishery: Catfish, tilapia, and aquaculture operations
  • Rabbitry: Small but growing niche market

Understanding Livestock Operations

Each livestock type has its own structure, input needs, revenue cycle, and risk level. Here’s a simplified breakdown:

Enterprise Revenue Cycle Key Inputs Common Risks
Broilers 6-8 weeks Day-old chicks, feed, vaccines, housing Disease outbreaks, feed price hikes
Layers 12-18 months Chicks, layer feed, lighting, medication Drop in egg production, disease
Cattle Long-term Grazing/pasture, water, vet care Theft, slow growth, poor nutrition
Fish 4-6 months Fingerlings, feed, water quality Water pollution, disease, theft

The Financial Angle

Livestock businesses deal with high variable costs, especially feed and health management. Accountants must keep an eye on:

  • Feed-to-weight gain ratio
  • Mortality rates and losses
  • Daily operational costs
  • Sales cycles (live animal vs processed)
  • Inventory (livestock counts, feed stock, meds)

For instance, in poultry farming, feed accounts for up to 70% of the total cost. If feed prices rise and aren’t managed properly, profit margins collapse quickly.

Opportunities in Livestock and Poultry

Nigeria’s growing population and urbanization are increasing demand for:

  • Chicken, turkey, and eggs (especially during festive seasons)
  • Milk and dairy products
  • Fish as a protein alternative
  • Processed meat products (sausages, frozen chicken, etc.)

There’s also rising interest in value addition:

  • Egg powder for bakeries
  • Dried fish and meat snacks
  • Dairy processing (yogurt, cheese, butter)

These open up space for small and medium agribusinesses that need accountants to guide expansion.

Key Areas Accountants Add Value

  • Cost tracking: Know the actual cost per egg, per kilogram of fish, or per broiler
  • Breakeven analysis: Especially important with volatile feed and medicine prices
  • Cash flow planning: Some livestock projects have delayed income cycles
  • Inventory and biological asset management: Especially for scaling operations
  • Profitability analysis: Compare processed vs live sales, breed performance, etc.
  • Insurance planning: Risk of disease and loss is real — mitigation matters

Ethical and Environmental Considerations

Modern agribusiness also requires awareness of:

  • Animal welfare standards
  • Proper waste management to avoid pollution
  • Traceability for food safety and export

Accountants should ensure record-keeping supports compliance in these areas.
